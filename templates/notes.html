GENERAL QUESTIONS:

Q1.Who decided which startups passed on the next stage?


A1.The teams that gathered the most investments by promoting their startup (not real money) , and only 9 passed onto the next stage


Q2.Out of how many startups does the winning projects pass?


A2.Well it depends on how many startups are there , for example last year out of 18 startups 8 passed , However this year out of 21 startups 9 passed


Q3.What is a startup?


A3.Startups are companies or ventures that are focused on a single product or service that the founders want to bring to market. These companies typically don't have a fully developed business model and, more crucially, lack adequate capital to move onto the next phase of business.


Q4.What happens after this stage?


A4.After this stage the students with the losing startups join the winning startups and they compete in the summer , in the end only 1 startup wins.


Q5.What are the most popular startups?


A5.There is no piece of information containing the most "popular" startup , However the last update that we recieved indicated that "Fortuna" was the most popular.


Q6.Can i work on a startup alone?


A6.In order to work on a startup alone you have to find the perfect balance between being a good Developer , Designer , Uxer and Product manager , but working in teams is always more efficient and less stressful.
